LI BAI

Background

Li Bai is an eternal romantic and individualist, a poet swordsman who can split a hair with his blade while
composing poetry that will be recited in courts across the land. Born in Alsahraa, he was well-versed in
the writings of Madinat Erudit's sages from an early age, which inspired his personal expression of
freedom. He spent much of his youth wandering the world at his leisure, developing a swordsmanship
that was as unpredictable as his whereabouts. However, after witnessing his hometown's destruction
and the framing of his friends, the swordsman embarked on a quest for truth and justice.

Lore

The majestic Chang'an has stood for hundreds of years. The gate of Chang'an City, however, the
impregnable Phoenix Gate, was engraved with a poem slashed into the stone by a young man with a
longsword: "My heart yearns for the blue sky and to embrace the bright moon," and it caused a sensation
throughout Chang'an. When magistrate Di Renjie volunteered to arrest him for vandalizing the city, the
Empress refused his offer and even ordered that the Will of the Sword poem be preserved. In just a few
days, the name of the young swordsman had spread throughout Chang'an City. His name was Li Bai.

At that time, Li Bai was young and frivolous. After refusing the Empress' invitation to become an official,
he began a journey to hone his swordsmanship. When he saw the surging Yellow River for the first time,
the Will of the Sword burst forth from within him, rushing to the sea, never to return.

With no machinery knowledge, sorcery techniques, or Ferali blood, Li Bai nevertheless became one of the
greatest in the empire, relying only on himself and his sword. He was a rare natural talent. He composed a
poem for each opponent he defeated, and each poem's name spread far and wide along with the name of
his sword. Later, some even sought defeat at his hands in order to be honored by one of his poems—a
startling turn of events.

Shortly after Li Bai's Will of the Sword reached its peak in popularity, his journey brought him to Alsahraa.
Few knew that Li Bai, who lived in Chang'an City, had actually been born in Alsahraa. He returned in the
glory of a sword master only to discover that the bustling city of his childhood no longer existed. It had
been abandoned, buried by the yellow sands.

This scene made Li Bai's spirit burn. Sword aloft, he barged back into Chang'an City and demanded an
explanation from the empress. The sword master stormed Daming Palace and shook Chang'an to its core.
For the first time in its history, Chang'an City was threatened by an ordinary person.

No one knows what happened in the end, but Li Bai withdrew from Chang'an. His private talks with the
empress were regarded as taboo and deliberately left out of historical records.

Only Li Bai himself knew that his pride had been thwarted at the peak of his strength. After that, he went
into self-imposed exile and searched for new meaning in his lonely journey.

Everyone thought the sword master dead, but in the Chang'an administrative office, Di Renjie looked at
reports of Li Bai's whereabouts in subtle contempt.

"What are your thoughts, Fang?"

Without waiting for an answer, he said to himself, "When he comes back, there will be even greater
destruction. The man is too full of himself, and he doesn't have anyone else."

The day Di Renjie foresaw came a few years later.

It was an ordinary day in Chang'an. The morning bell echoed against the dawn sky, and a traveler from
distant Alsahraa looked up at the sword strokes on the Phoenix Gate. Di Renjie's spies, hidden in the
shadows, watched nervously at the dashing figure in white.

And so sword master Li Bai had returned to Chang'an.

How would Chang'an be shaken this time?

"The River Blade, descending from the heavens!"
