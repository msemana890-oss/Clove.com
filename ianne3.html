ALLAIN

Background

Chilling dual swords. An innocent spirit. These two may seem in conflict, but somehow they exist in
perfect harmony in Allain. Bereft of his memories, he was forced to wander the streets of Navenia. While
blessed with remarkable swordsmanship, he was cheated time and time again due to his innocent nature.
To survive, he joined the Mercenary Guild. Now, he works hard to survive, his short-term goal being to fill
his belly, and his long-term goal being to save up enough for a boat ticket home.

Lore


As an orphan, Allain was fortunate enough to be taken in by the School of Steel and given the very best
training. Unlike the other children, Allain displayed a fondness for swordplay, and given his immense
talent, the instructors considered him a rising star.

"Remember, Allain: no matter what you want to do after you leave the School of Steel, use your sword to
protect."

Keeping his instructor's words close to his heart, Allain began to picture his future as a
skilled swordsman.

During his training, Allain met Butterfly, a blonde-haired fellow orphan. Allain was proud of his sword
skills, but he proved to be no match for Butterfly. He began to develop inexplicable feelings for her: he
wanted her to remain the strongest warrior she could be, but he also hoped to catch up with her so they
could become a fearsome pair of blade-wielders. Over the following years of mutual support and growth,
two developed a profound bond.

Allain and Butterfly were sent on their first mission upon reaching the age of seventeen. Unaware that a
traitor had sold them out, they walked straight into a Shadow Hand ambush. Seeking only to protect
Butterfly, Allain dove headlong into the fray, but after the dust had settled, his body was nowhere to be
found. By the time Allain reawakened, the Shadow Hand had transformed his body into human weaponry,
erasing his memories and nullifying his emotions. All he knew was that the organization had given him a
new life, a new blade, and a new mission—and for that simple reason, he was willing to serve them. During
his missions, however, Allain was exposed to increasingly nefarious and disturbing things, and the
unnerved Allain began to question his loyalty. The Shadow Hand discovered his reservations and planned
to wipe his memories once again. Realizing that something was afoot, Allain decided to flee, but he was
pursued by Shadow Hand assassins. Chased into dire straits, Allain fell into the sea and was never again
seen in his homeland.

Whether by fate's mercy or mockery, Allain awoke in a shipping crate in Navenia's Vanilla Dock, unable to
remember his prospective future, nor his time spent spilling blood for the sinister Shadow Hand. In the
only fragment of memory he could bring to mind, he recalled a blonde-haired swordswoman protecting
him, blocking countless flurries of blade slashes. As he fell into the sea, she was shouting at him: Allain.
He surmised that this must be his name, but he remained puzzled at where his two swords had come
from. Who was he? Where was he going? Allain didn't know, but he was determined to figure out why he
wielded his swords.
