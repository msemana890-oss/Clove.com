ZILONG

Background

Integrity and courage make up the foundation of Zilong's life. Even when traveling alone in troubled
times, his firm faith has never wavered. Growing up with the harsh realities of life in a mercenary group
shaped his bold stance, and he never failed to be at the frontlines, earning him the reputation of one who
"knows no fear." Now, with the companionship of like-minded people and his silver spear in his hand, he
has become an invincible force that is always moving towards higher ideals.

Lore

Since time immemorial, mercenaries have bounced between different masters, using their blood and
sweat to earn themselves a living. Crisis, danger, betrayal, and death were all in a day's work for them.
Today's friends might become tomorrow's enemies, and adversaries of the past might become allies of
the present.

And yet one stood out from the crowd. The man known as "Zilong" wasn't your average hired hitman. With
his charismatic leadership, he united and nurtured a ragtag bunch of outlaws into a powerful mercenary
group named "Dragon". Cao Cao had contracted Zilong's services several times, and seeing his mercenary
organization grow stronger and stronger, Cao Cao decided he'd like them to belong to him permanently.
Needless to say, Zilong refused.

This event kicked off an intricate plot. Thereafter, Cao Cao began to stoke the flame of Zilong's ambition.
Zilong eventually desired to abandon his status as a mere mercenary and instead become the one
standing above the rest. Killing the warlord Lu Bu would be the first step toward achieving this dream.

But someone in the organization opposed the idea. Known as "Shadow", he had long been Zilong's closest
friend and right-hand man. Unbeknownst even to Cao Cao, "Dragon" was in fact a cooperation between
these two men: the organization's namesake was responsible for their clientele and assignments, but
Shadow was the real, raw force behind the organization.

This was the first time the pair had ever had a serious disagreement. Shadow opposed Zilong's ambition
and did not believe that Cao Cao would fully support anyone who might eventually become a threat to
himself. Zilong, however, felt that he had everything under control. To Shadow, it was a massive gamble—
one that put the lives of all the group's members at stake. Unable to persuade Zilong, Shadow left the
organization.

Having lost his companion, Zilong decided to take Cao Cao's advice and use underhanded methods to
combat Lu Bu. Even though Lu Bu may have fallen for such schemes, the warlord still proved to be a
formidable force. All the tricks and traps in the world fall apart in the face of absolute, raw power. In the
blink of an eye, Lu Bu single-handedly slaughtered nearly the entire organization. At the crucial moment,
Shadow appeared and helped Zilong slay Lu Bu, unable to simply abandon his long-time friend. Thus,
they became the sole survivors of this bitter conflict.

After the dust settled, Shadow had disappeared. His whereabouts remained unknown until several years
later, when Cao Cao's army swept through the Three Kingdoms, only to suffer an unprecedented setback
at the Hills of Changban. It was there that a sky-splitting lightning bolt struck down the famous general
of which Cao Cao's army had been so proud of.

Shadow has perished, and today, the warrior on the battlefield is the silver-spear-wielding Zilong,
undefeated and fearless.

"This spear holds a power greater than lightning!"
