MUSASHI

Background

With a longsword in his left hand and a shortsword in his right, Musashi moved like a shadow on the
battlefield. He was lethal, yet never at ease. Though his technique was unmatched, his eyes betrayed a
soul haunted by every life he had taken. Fear clung to him, not of death, but of becoming the very
monster he was trained to defeat. He wandered the world, drawn to duels not out of pride but by a need
to quiet the voices of doubt and dread within. But while he was away, lost in a haze of steel and survival,
tragedy struck home. His childhood friend Otsu met a cruel end, one he hadn’t been there to stop.

The news shattered him. Paralyzed by guilt and grief, Musashi cast aside his swords and vanished into
obscurity for three years. He feared what he had become. He feared that his blade brought only sorrow.
Then, in the stillness of those years, Otsu’s spirit came to him. Not in anger, but with a gentle plea. With
her blessing, Musashi once again took up his blades. This time not to kill, but to grow. To fulfill her wish
that he become the greatest swordsman, not through fear or blood, but through mastery, peace, and
purpose.

Lore


Musashi grew up obsessed with the blade. From a young age, he aspired to reach the pinnacle of
swordsmanship and become the world's greatest samurai. This aspiration was hindered, however, by his
family's poverty. They could not afford to pay for training, so he was mercilessly kicked out of the dojo
every time he managed to find a way to sneak in. The kind-hearted owner of the sweets shop across the
street from the dojo took pity on Musashi, and he made a deal with the dojo master to let his tuition be
paid in sweets daily.

Whenever her daughter, Otsu, brought the sweets to the dojo, she always saw Musashi getting knocked
to the ground by a student much taller than him. But as soon as he saw her, he would leap up from the
ground and wave at her as if nothing had happened. As the days went by, the two became childhood
sweethearts.

One night, drunken hooligans started causing trouble at the sweets shop. Musashi appeared out of
nowhere, charging forward to protect Otsu with his wooden training swords. He managed to drive the
hooligans away but was injured in the process. As Otsu tended his wounds, feeling guilty for having
caused them, Musashi made a solemn promise. "Do not be afraid. I will become the world's greatest
samurai, and I will protect you."

Musashi took his oath to heart, training arduously and even developing his own Niten Ichiryuu technique.
He roamed Kyokutou, challenging every samurai he came across. Never losing a duel, his reputation
quickly grew.

One day, as Musashi was leaving home, Otsu handed him an amulet. She smiled and said, "Before every
duel, you must first declare your name. That way, no matter where I am, I'll hear news of you."

Legends began to circulate in Kyokutou about a dual-bladed samurai. His technique was exquisite, and
he introduced himself before every duel. "My name is Musashi, and I am the one who shall defeat you." No
one in Kyokutou could match his skills, putting him one step closer to the pinnacle to which he'd always
aspired.

But his contentment was soon uprooted. A powerful earthquake rocked Otsu's village. By the time he
arrived, Otsu was trapped beneath the rubble and gasping her final breaths. Otsu's death was a
devastating blow to Musashi. He sheathed his blades and left his hometown for good, seemingly
abandoning all the ideals he had once held so dear.

Otsu's spirit could not bear to watch Musashi devolve into despondence. In exchange for just one more
day with him, she paid a great price—her spirit would disappear forever. As Otsu appeared one final time
before Musashi, it was as if the shackles that bound his heart were shattered. He regained his fighting
spirit and remembered why he must take up his sword once more.

Musashi resumed his training and visited Li Bai, the most skilled swordsman of Chang'an. The two agreed
to duel each other in three years' time. To prepare for this world-famous duel, Musashi delved even
deeper into his training, challenging every master on the continent.

One day, a mysterious alchemist interrupted Musashi's training. In exchange for unconditional
assistance, the alchemist revealed to him an awful truth about that tragic earthquake—Otsu's death was
linked to the monsters known as the Blood Clan. Hearing this, Musashi vowed to solve the mystery of the
Blood Clan and avenge Otsu.

Every time Musashi destroyed a member of the wicked Blood Clan, he would cut a lock of hair from their
head and bring it back to their hometown. This was his way to give them back their dignity as human
beings.

Although it was a gamble of life and death, and despite the fact that his blades had been broken in
combat, Musashi still vowed to find Xu Fu—the villain who led the Blood Clan—and put an end to this
tragic tale once and for all.

Musashi grasps his reforged weapons and thinks only of Otsu as he scales the heights of swordsmanship
to fulfill his oath.

"Let me tell you a secret: I am invincible!"
