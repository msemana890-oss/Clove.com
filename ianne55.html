MARCOPOLO

Background

Marco Polo bears many titles—traveler, adventurer, polyglot... Free-spirited, intelligent, and
passionate, he has an insatiable thirst for knowledge. The intrepid lad was raised by his uncle
after losing his father at a young age. However, he also inherited a fragment of the ancient Arcana
from his father, which was the key to the origins of knowledge. Once he deciphered the information
pertaining to the source of knowledge, he followed in his father's footsteps, carrying his twin guns
to the distant eastern continent on his own quest for knowledge.

Lore

Traveler, adventurer, polyglot... People call him Marco Polo.

Born into a wealthy family, his father and uncle helmed an enormous merchant fleet, so they were
often away from home on long sea voyages. Unfortunately, the family was dwindling in number. While
Marco Polo was still a boy, a courier brought news that his father had made his final voyage and
would never return. Thereafter, Marco Polo was raised by his uncle, Maffeo.

Maffeo treated him as his own son and gave him the best education. The family estate was like a vast
library, packed with precious texts and rare manuscripts his father and uncle had brought back from all
over the world, as well as a wide array of complex mechanisms and intricately crafted antiques. Marco
Polo loved to listen to Maffeo's stories of the travels he had embarked on as a youth with his father, and
the boy enjoyed plotting their past journeys on his father's old maps. As he grew up, Marco Polo became
obsessed with disassembling the intriguing mechanisms and tuning them with great precision. Under the
tutelage of his master, he learned a great deal about the profound mechanical arts.

His master once spoke of some very special parchment scrolls. Excavated from ancient ruins and sold at
an exorbitant cost, these scrolls had taken the west by storm. Known as "Heavenly Scrolls" by easterners,
it was said that they contained lost knowledge about machinery. As an expert on machinery himself,
Marco Polo's master confirmed that the precious scrolls indeed contained the legendary key to the origin
of knowledge, which he hoped could be used to build mighty machinery. Unfortunately, this did not come
to pass, as the scrolls were soon lost.

Suddenly, it came to him like a bolt of lightning. Marco Polo realized that the merchant who had
purchased the scrolls was his father, yet he knew almost nothing about the man. He bid farewell to his
master and rushed home to search the estate, but found nothing. Dispirited, Marco Polo fell asleep
clutching his telescope. He dreamed of his father's departing silhouette and the courier who had brought
the fateful news... Marco Polo woke with a start, realizing that the courier had not actually said his father
was dead—only that he would never return. He abruptly remembered the little "toy"—that small shard his
father had given him just before leaving for the final time. It was an ancient engraved tablet.

Marco Polo inspected the tiny tablet with his sophisticated machinery. When light shone upon it from a
certain angle, writing appeared. It was a letter from his father, etched so many years before.

"By the time you read these words, my son, I will have passed from this world. Do not be sad. This is the
fate that every seeker of knowledge must accept. That said, leaving you has caused me endless pain and
much regret. Remember that I love you, and that I will always watch over you from across the other side
of the origin of knowledge."

A crowd had gathered before the bulletin board in front of the governor's estate, and the people shook
their heads at what they read. A recruitment notice had been posted, seeking to hire a fearless
adventurer for a journey to the far east. Having heard news of the bulletin, Maffeo rushed over only to
witness his nephew reach out and rip down the notice. He suddenly realized that Marco Polo had grown
into a poised young man, carrying his beloved twin pistols at his waist just as his father had at his age. As
tears ran down his wrinkled cheeks, Maffeo cried out to stop his nephew. No one knew better than him
what hardships would lie on the path to the origin of knowledge. Marco Polo simply turned to face his
uncle, then bowed gracefully to the crowd before proceeding without hesitation into the governor's
estate.

"An entire world is waiting to be discovered out there!"
