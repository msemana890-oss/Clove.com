CAOCAO

Background

"I would rather betray the world than let the world betray me." Cao Cao is willing to go to any lengths
necessary to achieve his ambitious desires. His experience with oppression by the elites in these troubled
times has taught him that relying on benevolence and virtue is futile in the face of corrupted power. Cao
Cao understands that if he wants to restore order in these turbulent times, he will have to fight fire with
fire—even if it costs him his name. Humanity is nothing more than pawns to him, and the massive sword
he wields is destined to be stained with the blood of anyone who gets in his way.

Lore

War is a game for Cao Cao, a game of ambition and desire. A game must have rules, and Cao Cao's desire
was to become the one who made those rules.

However, too many stood in his way— The Protector Fuzi and Jixia Academy; Jiang Ziya, an elusive man
skilled in sorcery and machinery; and Wu Zetian, who reached the height of power in Chang'an and the
empire... Cao Cao was forced to consider how he would undo the current rules of the game while
expanding his forces.

Soon, Cao Cao's cunning and ambition helped him achieve a victory. The Warlord Lu Bu —one who'd
always stood in his way— had fallen to his schemes and conspiracies, and he gained control over the
mysterious mercenary group, Dragon—though he was still unaware at the time that Zhao Yun, the man
called Shadow, was behind Dragon.

While expanding his forces, Cao Cao realized that machinery and sorcery were the powers that
dominated this world, and he needed a new source of power to overcome the order they maintained.

An opportunity presented itself when Xu Fu returned from Kyokutou and proposed working with Cao Cao
to rebuild his own forces. If Cao Cao provided prisoners of war to help Xu Fu rebuild the Blood Clan
Legion, then in exchange, Xu Fu would help Cao Cao unite the Three Kingdoms. But Cao Cao told Xu Fu
that he desired to become part of the Blood Clan instead.

Xu Fu let out a low and hoarse laugh when he heard this. He believed that Cao Cao had been bewitched
by the power he displayed and would end up a puppet under his control, just like Mi Yue.

The first thing Cao Cao did upon being converted into the Blood Clan was to create an accident. Cao Cao
killed Cai Yong and his people, who were proficient in machinery and sorcery yet refused to serve him.

The beast Dian Wei, whom Cai Yong had taken in and raised was deceived by Cao Cao's schemes and took
his side instead. As for Cai Yan, Cai Yong's darling daughter, her extraordinary talent would be used by
Cao Cao once she grew up.

Cao Cao started to put his plans into motion.

He openly proclaimed that he would use Cai Yong's research to search for weapons of mass destruction
from the ancient times. When his expedition led him to the Eastwind Altar, and he sent his army to the
Red Cliffs, everyone thought his goal was to unify the Three Kingdoms. As a result, Jiang and Yi felt
threatened, ultimately leading to the formation of an alliance between them, and so their entire focus
converged on the Eastwind Altar. But none of them realized that it was just a distraction.

Cao Cao sought something much greater and shocking, and it was the first step in his plan to overturn
the current order in the world.

On a small island near the Eastern Sea, Xu Fu carefully carved another masterpiece —after Bai Qi— that
made him proud. The Warlord Lu Bu who once shocked the continent was being resurrected in secret.
That was Cao Cao's true trump card.

Xu Fu was immersed in his craft of blood and evolution, yet he overlooked Cao Cao's nature...

Cao Cao was a man who could play his enemies with easy, so he naturally didn't mind sacrificing someone
on his side to achieve his own goals. His ambition didn't just stop at uniting the Three Kingdoms, even Xu
Fu, who was hiding ambitions of his own, would be eliminated. If the plan succeeded, Cao Cao would take
full control of the Blood Clan.

It was a grand game of strategy.

"I would rather betray the world than let the world betray me."
