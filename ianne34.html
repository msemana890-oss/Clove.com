SHOUYUE

Background

His fellow Great Wall Guards know him as the sharpest marksman in the regiment. Coolheaded and warm
in equal measure, he reliably provides cover during battles. Yet during calm times, his sharp eyes will gaze
longingly into the distance toward his hometown. When he was little, he was separated from his younger
brother, Xuance, during a bandit attack on their village, and Shouyue has always blamed himself for
failing to protect him. Shouyue thus waits by the Great Wall, partially in the hope that his younger brother
will eventually have sense enough to look for him there.

Lore

"I'll look after my mother and little brother."

As his father took his last breath beneath the Great Wall, the boy picked up his rifle, trembling from head
to toe.

"I'll always protect my brother. Always."

This was the vow the boy made to his dying mother, as she clasped the hands of her two sons with all her
might. And it was this vow that made the boy a man.

Without a mother or father, the two children grew up in a town near the Great Wall with just each other.
The young man looked after his little brother and made a living for them by doing odd jobs. He was quick-
witted and always had a smile on his face, so people happily helped him. In his spare time, the elder
brother would leave his younger brother with a kind neighbor and go out hunting by himself. He knew all
the fertile lands both near and far and would always come back with a full bounty.

His only worry was how to teach these skills to his little brother. The young man listened carefully to his
neighbor's grandmother teaching her three grandchildren; thus, he learned how to cook so that his picky
brother would still grow big and strong. He carved wooden figurines and toys for his little brother. He even
learned difficult skills like sewing clothes, which was harder to him than firing at prey from 100 meters
away.

Xuance, the chubby-cheeked little brother who had also lost his parents, was well taken care of by his big
brother, but he grew into a shy and sensitive child. He was afraid of thunder, afraid of being laughed at,
afraid of talking to strangers, and even afraid to speak to his big brother sometimes. There seemed to be
a split growing between the two.

The young man returned from hunting one day to find that there had been a misunderstanding. Xuance
thought that his older brother had abandoned him. Hysterical, he had charged through town to their
parents' graves crying, "Even my big brother left me!"

"Never!" The young man crouched in front of Xuance, stroking his little brother's head. "Your big brother
will never leave you."

To make it up to his little brother, the young man carved him a wooden figurine that looked just like him.

Xuance took out his own figurine, in which one could vaguely make out a likeness of the young man. The
delicate boy and the rough boy were placed together, as if they were holding hands.

The young man made a promise: "We brothers will never be separated!"

But fate has a way of shattering expectations. A hoard of horse thieves attacked the town while the gates
were open for market, instantly engulfing the bustling scene in a sea of flames, and the townspeople
gathered to help the Guardians close the gates and protect their town. The young man wanted to join the
townspeople, but he also wanted to stay and look after Xuance. He was torn.

"Go," Xuance said as he climbed into a water tank to hide. Looking at his brother with tears in his eyes, he
said, "I'll wait for you at home."

"It's a promise, then."

"To defend the gates is to defend my brother." This is what he told himself.

When the battle was finally over, the young man went back home, but all he found was the wooden
figurine, broken and lying in the corner of the courtyard...

The neighbors told him that thieves had kidnapped children and the elderly and held them as hostages to
make their escape. Xuance had been hiding in the water tank, but had rushed out to protect them...

The promise had been broken.

Thereafter, there was a quiet figure among the Great Wall Guardians. He was a skilled marksman and well-
versed in the cooking and survival skills needed in the desert. His commanding officer's only problem was
that he was too active on missions.

He changed his name to Shouyue, because "Shouyue" means to keep your word. He uses this name to
remind himself that he must always keep his word.

Shouyue now searches farther and farther afield. He still firmly believes that his little brother is waiting for
him somewhere, and he'll always be there to welcome him back to their hometown.

"I always keep my promises."
